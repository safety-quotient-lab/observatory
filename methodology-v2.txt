1. SCOPE
--------

Every evaluated website receives a full 31-row matrix: Preamble + Articles
1-30. This is the same approach used for kashifshah-net in v1, now applied
uniformly to all sites.

Each cell in the matrix contains three values:
  - Compatibility score
  - Evidence strength
  - Notes (citing specific on-site content)

1a. Source Constraint: Site Content Only

  The evaluation is based EXCLUSIVELY on the website's own content --
  what the site itself presents, publishes, hosts, and states. This
  includes:

    INCLUDED (on-domain content):
      - Published pages, articles, posts, and media on the domain
      - Terms of service, privacy policies, community guidelines
      - About pages, mission statements, value statements
      - User-facing features, UI design, and interaction patterns
      - Help documentation, FAQs, support pages
      - Accessibility features and language availability
      - Cookie banners, consent flows, data controls
      - Content moderation policies (as stated on the site)
      - Public transparency reports hosted on the site
      - Site structure: what is free vs. paywalled
      - Advertising presence and type (on-site observation)
      - Login/registration requirements and data requested
      - Subdomains of the evaluated domain (e.g., blog.example.com)

    EXCLUDED (off-domain and external):
      - Content on other domains, even if linked from the site
      - Third-party investigations (HRW, Amnesty, etc.)
      - News reporting about the site
      - Court filings, lawsuits, regulatory actions
      - Employee accounts, whistleblower reports
      - Academic research about the site
      - Government reports or audits
      - Ranking indices (RDR, Freedom House, etc.)
      - Social media commentary about the site
      - Social media profiles on other platforms
      - Linked communities, forums, or content on other domains

    OPTIONAL TOGGLE -- Off-domain linked content [DEFAULT: OFF]:
      When enabled, the following may be considered as supplementary
      evidence (scored separately or noted alongside on-domain scores):
      - Content on platforms linked from the site (social profiles,
        communities, repositories, portfolios)
      - The existence of the link itself is always on-domain evidence
        (i.e., the fact that /social/ links to a human rights community
        is on-domain; the community's content is off-domain)
      When disabled (default), only on-domain content is scored. The
      presence of outbound links may be noted but the linked content
      is not evaluated.

  Rationale: This evaluates what the site SAYS and SHOWS, not what
  outsiders report about it. A site's own content is the most direct
  evidence of its expressed values and user-facing practices. External
  sources measure behavior and impact; on-site content measures intent
  and presentation.

  Consequence: Sites may score differently under v2 than v1. A site
  with documented labor abuses (v1: negative) but no labor-related
  content (v2: ND) will diverge. This is expected and intentional --
  the two methodologies answer different questions:

    v1: "How does this site's RECORD align with the UDHR?"
    v2: "How does this site's OWN CONTENT align with the UDHR?"

2. SCORING SCALE
-----------------

2a. Compatibility Score

  Range: [-1.0, 1.0]

    -1.0  Site content actively contradicts this UDHR provision
    -0.5  Site content shows significant tension with this provision
     0.0  Site content is genuinely neutral on this provision
     0.5  Site content is generally compatible with this provision
     1.0  Site content strongly advances this UDHR provision
     ND   No relevant on-site content found; cannot score

  Scores may use any increment (e.g., -0.3, 0.7). The five named points
  above are anchors, not the only valid values.

  Key distinction:
    0.0 = We examined the site and its content is neutral on this point.
    ND  = The site has no content relevant to this provision.

  Examples:
    A privacy policy that exists but says nothing notable about
    data minimization: 0.0 on Art. 12.
    A sports site with no privacy policy or data-related pages
    visible: ND on Art. 12.
    A privacy policy that commits to data minimization and gives
    users deletion controls: positive on Art. 12.
    A privacy policy that claims broad rights to sell user data
    with no opt-out: negative on Art. 12.

2b. Evidence Strength

  Each score is paired with an evidence strength indicator:

    H   High    -- Multiple on-site pages/sections directly address
                   this provision (e.g., dedicated policy page, mission
                   statement, and feature implementation all align)
    M   Medium  -- At least one on-site page or feature directly
                   addresses this provision
    L   Low     -- Inferred from site design, tone, or indirect signals
                   (e.g., absence of tracking suggests privacy respect,
                   but no explicit policy found)

  ND scores do not carry an evidence strength.

5. AGGREGATE METRICS
---------------------

Per-site aggregates computed from the 31 scores:

  - Mean score (excluding ND)
  - Mean of non-zero scores (excluding ND and 0.0)
  - Max score and which article
  - Min score and which article
  - Count of negative scores
  - Count of ND scores
  - Sections with signal (non-zero, non-ND)
  - Evidence profile (count of H/M/L ratings)

Cross-site aggregates:

  - Per-article mean across all sites
  - Per-article worst/best site
  - Overall mean, median, standard deviation
  - Tier breakdown
  - Regional/country patterns

7. PROCESS
----------

  a) Fetch and review the site's publicly accessible content:
       - Homepage and primary navigation
       - Privacy policy / data practices page
       - Terms of service / user agreement
       - About / mission / values pages
       - Community guidelines / content policy
       - Accessibility statement
       - Transparency reports (if hosted on-site)
       - Help / support documentation
       - Any human rights, sustainability, or ethics pages
  b) For each of the 31 UDHR sections, assess whether on-site content
     is relevant, and if so, score it
  c) Assign score + evidence strength + notes per section
  d) Notes must cite specific on-site pages or features as evidence
  e) Compute per-site aggregates
  f) After all sites scored, compute cross-site aggregates
  g) Consistency check: verify similar sites scored similarly

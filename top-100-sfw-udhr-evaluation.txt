NOTES
-----

1. State-controlled platforms (Russia, China) score worst because they
   are structurally incompatible with UDHR principles -- they function
   as instruments of state censorship and surveillance.

2. Western Big Tech scores moderately negative because companies face
   structural conflicts between ad-based business models (vs Art. 12),
   government compliance demands (vs Art. 19), and supply chain
   economics (vs Art. 4, 23, 24).

3. The highest-scoring sites share common traits: free access, minimal
   data collection, editorial independence, and mission alignment with
   knowledge sharing or privacy protection.

4. 2024-2025 shows a broad industry retreat from human rights
   commitments: Meta rolled back hate speech policies, Google reversed
   its AI weapons ban, multiple companies cut DEI programs, and no
   company scored above 50% in the 2025 Ranking Digital Rights index.

5. The average score of -0.20 for the world's most-visited websites
   suggests that the dominant internet infrastructure operates in
   mild tension with UDHR principles, driven primarily by
   surveillance capitalism, authoritarian compliance, and supply
   chain exploitation.
